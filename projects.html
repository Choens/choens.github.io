Below is a collection of some of my current and ongoing projects in alphabetical order. All links (I do like links) will open in a separate tab. Most of the project listed here are licensed under a copy-left license.

1st Friday Albany Website:

I wrote and maintain the 1st Friday Albany website for the Upstate Artists Guild (I am a board member and code-monkey.) 1st Friday Albany is a monthly regional arts event. By working with Albany business, churches, nonprofits and organizations, the UAG is able to promote a monthly night of art for the Capital District. We are the first and oldest such art night in the Capital District.

The website is actually static. It is a custom Jekyll theme I built using Zurb Foundation 6 . The current 1st Friday Albany page went live in early January, 2016 and has met our needs well thus far. Unlike the previous page, it is fully responsive and looks good on phones, tablets, and large-screen monitors. In the future, I intend to replace it with something more dynamic and sophisticated, but I appreciate and enjoy the simplicity of being able to write a sophisticated page, that is nothing by HTML, CSS and Javascript (assembled by Ruby).

If anyone would like to volunteer to help me with this project, I am accepting pull requests.

Blood Sugars Project:

This is a analytic project I started in 2015, to use my wife's CGM data to predict future blood glucose levels. This project has been in hiatus for several months, but I intend to resurrect it at some point in 2016.

You can track this project on its own page: https://choens.github.io/blood-sugars/ . Eventually, I intend to produce a CGM-driven Shiny portal Karen can use when she goes to see her endocrinologist, but that is probably several months away.

Titanic Introduction To R:

A series of web-based presentations, code, and data, I wrote to help me teach the fundamentals of R programming. Participants are assumed to have completed a college-level statistics course, but are not expected to have programming experience. I assume participants have little to no programming experience outside of languages such as SPSS and SAS, which tend to encourage bad habits.

The materials are available here: http://choens.github.io/titanic/ . The website is built using Jekyll (using the excellent theme)and Reveal.js . Feel free to re-use this material. If you do, please drop me a quick email and let me know how it went. If anyone would like to volunteer to help me maintain or extend this material, I am accepting pull requests.
