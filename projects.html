1st Friday Albany Website

I wrote and maintain the 1st Friday Albany website for the Upstate Artists Guild. Working with local business, churches, non-profits, etcetera, the UAG promotes a monthly arts night in the Capital District. We are the first and oldest such arts night in the Capital District and we turn 10 in September, 2016!

In addition to being the UAG's official code monkey, I am a board member (in charge of web-stuff) and promote the 1st Friday Albany and UAG brands on social media.

In terms of tech, the 1st Friday website is a static Jekyll site, using a custom Zurb Foundation 6 theme. This incarnation of the 1st Friday Albany website went live early January of 2016 and has met our needs, thus far. Unlike the previous page, it is fully responsive (looks good on phones, tablets, and large-screen monitors). Going forward, I will replace it with something more dynamic or port it to Hexo or Perlican, to reduce the amount of Ruby I have to work with. But frankly, I enjoy the simplicity of a static site.

If anyone would like to volunteer to help me with this project, I am accepting pull requests.

Blood Sugars Project

This is a analytical project I started in 2015, to better understand my wife's Type 1 diabetes. Eventually, I would like to use her CGM data to predict future blood glucose levels. This project has been in hiatus for several months, but I intend to resurrect it at some point in late 2016. Karen has been using a different CGM, which doesn't work with Nightscout and the baby (Monkey!) has reduced the amount of time I have available for side projects.

You can track this project on its own page: https://choens.github.io/blood-sugars/ . Eventually, I intend to produce a CGM-driven Shiny portal Karen can use when she goes to see her endocrinologist, but that is probably several months away.

NSW

A reusable set of materials to help me explain and teach co-workers about Propensity Score Matching, as a improvement over using simple linear regressions to control for differences between the intervention group and the non-scientific control group. This is a new project (started August, 2016) and with my recent reductions in free time, may take a while to get finished.

Titanic Introduction To R (TITR)

A turn-key workshop, because I enjoy teaching analyst and students how to use R. It is a series of presentations (reveal.js), code (.R), and data (CSV), I use to teach the fundamentals of R programming. I assume participants have little to no programming experience outside of languages such as SPSS and SAS, which tend to encourage bad habits.

The materials are available here: http://choens.github.io/titanic/ . The website is built using Jekyll (using the excellent Lanyon theme)and Reveal.js . Feel free to re-use this material. If you do, please drop me a quick email and let me know how it went. If anyone would like to volunteer to help me maintain or extend this material, I am accepting pull requests.
